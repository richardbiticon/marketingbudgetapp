# The MMC Rebuild

The plan to rebuild the marketing execution team: five initial hires, a permanent monthly recruiting engine, paid and free test tasks, and an onboarding chain. This page is the single source of truth. Edit it as the plan evolves.

## The Push

The push has three parts that move together.

**1. Team rebuild.** Five roles, hired in sequence: Marketing Operations Coordinator, Social Media Specialist, Senior Graphic Designer, Junior Graphic Designer, and Video Editor. Each role gets a designed opportunity in the Builder, a public post, a tracker, and a test task before any offer.

**2. Tools.** Every hire works inside the Marketing OS from day one: the calendar, the budget, this room. The OS is the system of record. If it is not in the OS, it did not happen.

**3. Content overflow.** The team exists to produce volume: social posts, email sends, landing pages, and video. The pipeline is designed so output scales with headcount instead of with founder hours.

## What It Costs

Monthly payroll target for the full five-person team lands between $4,500 and $7,200 depending on seniority and ramp. Recruiting costs are small and recurring: job post fees, paid test tasks, and the hours to review applicants. Numbers live on each position card and roll up in Budget.

## Where It Comes From

The marketing budget carries the team. The reallocation work in Budget frees the room: trim diminishing-return ad spend first, and protect anything touching Team revenue. Each hire must map to output that either makes money or frees the hours of someone who does.

## The Process

1. **Interview first.** A short call. We check communication, portfolio honesty, and hunger. No homework before this call.
2. **Paid or free test.** A scoped task that mirrors the real job. Senior roles get a paid test. Entry roles may get a short free test, kept under two hours.
3. **Offer by email.** Plain terms: pay, ramp, cadence, and the ninety-day goals. No surprises later.

## The Timeline

Month one: Marketing Operations Coordinator and Social Media Specialist. Month two: Senior Graphic Designer. Month three: Junior Graphic Designer and Video Editor. The order can shift if an exceptional candidate shows up out of sequence. The bench exists for exactly that.

## The Monthly Cadence

Recruiting never stops. Every month: refresh the posts, review new applicants into the tracker, run at least two interviews per open or bench role, and add strong people to the bench even when nothing is open. The bench is the warm pool we call first.
